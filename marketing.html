The Launch Plan

The room gets filled through message, proof, urgency, and follow-up.

Pretty & Paid is not built on random posting. The launch strategy moves women from awareness to interest to a real registration decision.

Campaign Arc

The six-week sequence turns the room from idea into momentum.

Week 1

Disrupt the assumptions

Show women that a room can be built without waiting on perfect conditions.

Week 2

Build authority

Share founder story, proof, and room credibility so the message feels trustworthy.

Week 3

Introduce the offer

Reveal the room, the promise, the investment, and the limited-seat invitation.

Week 4

Handle objections

Address money, confidence, readiness, and fear directly instead of hoping women ignore them.

Week 5

Create urgency

Use timing, seat count, and proof to help undecided women make a choice.

Week 6

Close and carry momentum forward

Finish the cohort well and direct future interest into a waitlist or next room.

Message Types

Every strong launch balances belief, authority, and invitation.

Belief-Shifting Content

Correct the lie that a woman needs money, structure, or celebrity before she can begin.

Authority Content

Show proof, room philosophy, experience, and why the method works in real life.

Conversion Content

Make the invitation clear, the next step obvious, and the urgency easy to understand.

Direct Follow-Up

The highest-converting conversations often happen in replies, DMs, and personal follow-up.

Sample Content Direction

The launch tone should sound like the room: elegant, direct, and deeply clear.

Founder Story

“I built rooms before I had perfect conditions.”

Use story to disarm the belief that only already-established women can host meaningful events.

Short-Form Video

“Three lies keeping women from building the room.”

Create content that is strong enough to save, share, and start conversations in comments and DMs.

Email

“What if the conference didn’t come out of your pocket?”

Use email to handle nuance, objection work, urgency, and direct response.

Proof

Show the atmosphere, not just the curriculum.

Women should be able to feel the room before they ever step into it.
